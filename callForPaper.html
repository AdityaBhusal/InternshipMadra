INSTRUCTIONS

Overview


From the more recent international experiences, Nepal can identify the immense opportunities and socio-economic potentials from the digital revolution and changing lives and societies with unprecedented speed and scale. Further, the digital solutions help to expedite achieving the SDGs on the time plan of the Government of Nepal. In this context, the Nepal Digital Association, Innovative Circle, and MaDRA are planning to jointly organize Nepal Digital Economy Summit -2024. The summit is a pioneering event to foster meaningful collaboration among governments, businesses, development partners, academia, and communities to leverage digital economy technologies, trends, and innovations. The event promises to engage the diverse stakeholders to understand and improve their capacities through discussions on research papers on several themes informing the digital economy of Nepal. The summit will broadly cover the future of digital economy elements through papers on various sectors - health and education (SDG-3 and 4), tourism, environment and climate change (SDG-13), digital public infrastructure, agriculture (SDG-2), financial institutions, financing and investment, legal and regulatory, and energy sector (SDG-7 and 9). The organizers expect the summit to impact sectoral priorities and strategies for promoting the digital economy, develop the milestones for the digitization process and promote the dialogues for ICT implications such as the utilization of disruptive technologies like AI, Blockchain, IoT, Cybersecurity, Data, among others. for enhancing the capacities of the stakeholders to construct e- commerce measures, design and develop the relevant policies and legal frameworks. As UNCTAD (2019) reported on the requirement of deepening the digital economy divide, the organizers anticipate the summit will foster dialogues and discussions to overcome the digital challenges of sustainable development goals (digital SDGs) set by the National Planning Commission, Nepal.

Objectives


The overall objective of the summit is to foster collaboration, explore sectoral innovation, and build up sustainable partnerships among all stakeholders for leveraging the digital economy through digital economy technologies to aid in overall socio- economic development, LDC graduation, SDG 2030, and international commitments.
The specific objectives are:
  • To garner a common understanding of the scope, importance, opportunities and threats of the digital economy in all sectors of development
  • To identify the major constraints for digital expansion in the country,
  • To assess the current status of sectoral digital SDGs and digital economy for advancing the digital infrastructures in Nepal
  • To build strong collaboration among the stakeholders to foster their partnerships for sectoral innovation and leverage digital technology for sustainable development

Sectoral Coverage and Themes


The Summit aims to cover the sectors that have clear significance and impact on the country’s advancement through digital technologies. Although the list is not exhaustive, the Summit aims to cover
  1. Digital Public Infrastructure
  2. Trasnportation
  3. Energy
  4. Tourism
  5. Health
  6. Education
  7. Agriculture
  8. Finance and Investments
  9. Environment and Climate Change
  10. Labour among others

The significance of the identified sector is growing over time. Recognizing their impact on the digital economy, the organizers intend to focus the sectoral priorities and strategies for digitization progress at the forefront of innovation and development and to integrate a diverse range of research papers that broadly explore digitization processes and issues to address the challenges and opportunities in Nepal.

Preferred Themes for the Paper


1. Strategic Sectors and Themes

1.1 Digital Policy and Governance

  • Stakeholder roles, responsibilities, and collaboration
  • Digital policy vision, goals, and outcomes
  • Legal frameworks and regulatory aspects

1.2 Digital Strategy and Planning

  • Strategic outcomes and intervention design
  • Sector-specific digital applications and services
  • Interoperability, standardization, and data governance
  • Change management, workforce development, and digital literacy

1.3 Digital Service

  • Tourism and hospitality
  • Education and elearning
  • Agriculture and smart farming
  • Finance, investments, and digital banking
  • Health and telemedicine
  • Labor and the digital workforce
  • Transport and smart mobility
  • Energy and smart grids
  • Environment and climate change monitoring

2. Digital Infrastructure Themes

2.1 Critical and Public Digital Infrastructure

  • Master incubators and accelerators
  • Digital industrial corridors and special economic zones
  • Sector specific infrastructure and incubation centers

2.2 Digital Public Infrastructure

  • Open Health Information Exchange (Open HIE)
  • Unified Payment Interface (UPI)
  • Unified Health Interface (UHI)

2.3 Sector Specific Infrastructure

  • Transport and logistics infrastructure
  • Energy infrastructure and smart grids

3. Economic and Financial Themes

3.1 Cost Benefit Analysis

  • Cost reduction strategies and optimization
  • Enterprise architecture (government, sector, industry, and program-specific)
  • Cost estimation and digital system development lifecycle
  • Uniform pricing for digital goods and services
  • Investment portfolio development and management
  • Finance, financing mechanisms, and investments

4. Cross Cutting Themes

4.1 Export and Internationalization

  • Sector-specific export of digital products and services
  • Institutional setup for digital sector export promotion
  • Creating and promoting Brand Nepal in the global digital marketplace

4.2 Research and Development

  • Standardization and interoperability labs
  • Academic integration and collaboration
  • Digital workforce development, job creation, and labor market implications
  • Digital solutions for sustainability, climate action, and resilience

Note:

➔ Papers based on the above themes are encouraged.
➔ Multidisciplinary studies and policy contexts will be highly valued.
➔ Please emphasize case studies, best practices, and policy recommendations where relevant.

Abstract and Full Paper Submission:

  • Authors are invited to submit abstracts (300-500 words) that address one or more of the sub-themes.
  • Abstracts should be submitted via email to info@ndesummit.org by November 15, 2024.
  • Notification of acceptance (with review feedback) will be sent by November 22, 2024.
  • The full paper submission deadline is December 5, 2024.
  • Full papers should be submitted after incorporating all the feedback and suggestions from the reviewer.

Submission Guidelines


  1. Paper submissions should be in MS Word
  2. Papers submitted to NDESummit 2024 should be original and not published or submitted to any other conference/journal
  3. Papers must be presented in English language
  4. The papers that are simultaneously submitted to this conference and other events or publication venues as well as submissions that do not utilize the correct formatting template may not be accepted
  5. Submissions will be selected based on their originality, timeliness, significance, relevance, and clarity of presentation

Document Setup

  • Page Size: A4 or Letter (depending on your region)
  • Margins: 1 inch on all sides
  • Font:
    • Body Text: Times New Roman or Arial, 12-point font
    • Headings: 14-point bold for main headings; 12-point bold for subheadings
  • Line Spacing: 1.5 or double-spaced
  • Paragraph Indentation: First Line indented by 0.5 inch
  • Body Text: Left-Aligned for body text; center-aligned for the title

Additional Tips

  • Page Numbers: Insert page numbers in the footer, centered or right-aligned
  • Headers/Footers: Include the title of the paper in the header (optional)
  • Footnotes: Use footnotes for additional commentary or citations (if needed)
  • Tables and Figures: Label and number tables/figures, and include captions

Peer Review

All accepted papers submitted for publication will be checked with anti-plagiarism software to check the overlapping/similarity index of the content with already published material. The papers with high overlapping indexes will be rejected straightaway. All other submissions will be sent for double-blind review by the Program Committee on the basis of technical quality, relevance to the conference’s topics of interest, originality, significance, and clarity. Submissions failing to comply with paper formatting and authors’ anonymity will be rejected without reviews.

Final Notification

The notification will be emailed to the author based on the reviewers’ feedback. The Notification will be e-mailed to authors under any of the three categories
1. Accepted [The corresponding author will receive an official acceptance letter as an attachment with the Email]
2. May be accepted with minor/major changes
3. Rejected

Editorial Policies

The authors should abide by the guidelines mentioned below before submitting the paper, if any dispute arises during peer review or after acceptance and publication, and if authors are unable to give a valid explanation, the NDES team retains the right to remove a submission from the editing process.
Plagiarism

● Plagiarism, image manipulation, and data fabrication are not tolerated.
● Plagiarism includes copying research ideas, text, images, or data from another source/research literature, even from the author’s own publications without giving any credit to the source.
● Text copied from another source must be reused within quotations, and the source must be referenced. If the design of the research, or the structure or language of the publication, was inspired by previous research, the corresponding works must be explicitly cited.
● If plagiarism is detected during the peer review process, the manuscript will be rejected. If plagiarism is detected after publication, we will retract the paper
